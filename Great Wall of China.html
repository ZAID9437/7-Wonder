WELCOME TO Great Wall of China (China)



1.History of Great Wall of China

→ The history of the Great Wall of China began when fortifications built by various states during the Spring and Autumn (771–476 BC)[1] and Warring States periods (475–221 BC) were connected by the first emperor of China, Qin Shi Huang, to protect his newly founded Qin dynasty (221–206 BC) against incursions by nomads from Inner Asia. The walls were built of rammed earth, constructed using forced labour, and by 212 BC ran from Gansu to the coast of southern Manchuria.

→ Later dynasties adopted different policies towards northern frontier defense. The Han (202 BC – 220 AD), the Northern Qi (550–574), the Jurchen-ruled Jin (1115–1234), and particularly the Ming (1369–1644) were among those that rebuilt, re-manned, and expanded the Walls, although they rarely followed Qin's routes. The Han extended the fortifications furthest to the west, the Qi built about 1,600 kilometres (990 mi) of new walls, while the Sui mobilised over a million men in their wall-building efforts. Conversely, the Tang (618–907), the Song (960–1279), the Yuan (1271–1368), and the Qing (1644–1912) mostly did not build frontier walls, instead opting for other solutions to the Inner Asian threat like military campaigning and diplomacy.

→ AAlthough a useful deterrent against raids, at several points throughout its history the Great Wall failed to stop enemies, including in 1644 when the Qing troops marched through the gates of the Shanhai Pass and replaced the most ardent of the wall-building dynasties, the Ming, as rulers of China proper.

→ The Great Wall of China visible today largely dates from the Ming dynasty, as they rebuilt much of the wall in stone and brick, often extending its line through challenging terrain.[2] Some sections remain in relatively good condition or have been renovated, while others have been damaged or destroyed for ideological reasons,[3] deconstructed for their building materials,[3] or lost due to the ravages of time.[4] For long an object of fascination for foreigners, the wall is now a revered national symbol and a popular tourist destination.[5]

2.Facts of Great Wall of China

→ The Great Wall of China is the collective name of a series of fortification systems generally built across the historical northern borders of China to protect and consolidate territories of Chinese states and empires against various nomadic groups of the steppe and their polities. Several walls were being built from as early as the 7th century BC by ancient Chinese states; selective stretches were later joined together by Qin Shi Huang (220–206 BC), the first Emperor of China. Little of the Qin wall remains. Later on, many successive dynasties have built and maintained multiple stretches of border walls. The most well-known sections of the wall were built by the Ming dynasty (1368–1644).

3.Best Time to Visit Great Wall of China

→ The best time to visit the Great Wall is in spring and autumn, especially from April to October. The weather is pleasant with moderate temperatures. In spring, the flowers are blooming, and in autumn, the leaves are turning red and yellow. The Great Wall is crowded during the Chinese national holidays, such as the National Day Holiday (October 1st to 7th) and the Labor Day Holiday (May 1st to 3rd). It is best to avoid these holidays if you want to enjoy a peaceful visit.

→ The Great Wall is open all year round, but the opening hours vary in different sections. The opening hours are usually from 8 am to 5 pm. Some sections are open until 6 pm in summer. The opening hours are extended during the peak season. The opening hours are shorter in winter. It is best to check the opening hours before you go.

→ The Great Wall is less crowded in winter, but it is cold and windy. The Great Wall is covered with snow in winter, which makes it more beautiful. It is best to wear warm clothes and shoes if you visit the Great Wall in winter.

→ The Great Wall is less crowded in the early morning and late afternoon. It is best to visit the Great Wall in the early morning or late afternoon if you want to avoid the crowds.

4.How to Reach Great Wall of China

→ The Great Wall of China is located in northern China, near Beijing. The most popular sections of the Great Wall are near Beijing, such as Badaling, Mutianyu, and Jinshanling. The Great Wall is about 70 kilometers (43 miles) from Beijing city center. It takes about 1.5 to 2 hours to drive from Beijing to the Great Wall. You can take a bus, taxi, or private car to get to the Great Wall from Beijing.

→ The Great Wall is well connected by public transportation. You can take a bus or train to get to the Great Wall from Beijing. There are direct buses from Beijing to the Great Wall. The buses depart from Deshengmen Bus Station in Beijing. The buses run from early morning to late afternoon. The journey takes about 1.5 to 2 hours. The bus fare is about 12 to 20 RMB. You can also take a train to get to the Great Wall from Beijing. The trains depart from Beijing North Railway Station. The trains run from early morning to late afternoon. The journey takes about 1.5 to 2 hours. The train fare is about 6 to 10 RMB.

→ The Great Wall is also well connected by private transportation. You can take a taxi or private car to get to the Great Wall from Beijing. The taxi fare is about 300 to 500 RMB. The private car fare is about 500 to 800 RMB. It is best to hire a local driver to take you to the Great Wall. The driver can speak English and help you with the tickets and directions.

5.Tips for Visiting Great Wall of China

→ The Great Wall is a popular tourist destination, so it is best to visit the Great Wall early in the morning or late in the afternoon to avoid the crowds. The Great Wall is less crowded in the early morning and late afternoon.

→ The Great Wall is long and steep, so it is best to wear comfortable shoes and clothes. It is best to wear hiking shoes and clothes if you want to hike the Great Wall. The Great Wall is made of stone and brick, so it is slippery and uneven in some sections.

→ The Great Wall is exposed to the sun and wind, so it is best to wear a hat and sunglasses. It is best to bring sunscreen and lip balm if you visit the Great Wall in summer. The Great Wall is covered with snow in winter, so it is best to wear warm clothes and shoes.

→ The Great Wall is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, so it is best to respect the rules and regulations. It is best to stay on the designated paths and not to climb the walls. It is best to take care of the environment and not to litter.

→ The Great Wall is a symbol of China, so it is best to learn about the history and culture of the Great Wall. It is best to take a guided tour to learn about the history and culture of the Great Wall. The guides can speak English and help you with the tickets and directions.
